Entity SEO

Entity SEO: The Foundation Every AI Visibility Strategy is Built On

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 8 min read

Entity SEO is the practice of building a clear, consistent, and corroborated digital identity for your business across all platforms that AI systems use as data sources. Without strong entity signals, AI systems cannot confidently identify or recommend your business, regardless of how good your website or content is.

What Is an Entity in SEO?

In SEO and AI systems, an entity is a distinct, identifiable thing: a person, place, organisation, or concept. Your business is an entity. For AI systems to recommend your business, they must be able to identify it as a distinct entity with clear, consistent attributes.

Entity attributes include: your business name, location, service category, operating hours, contact details, and key people. When these attributes are consistent across all platforms, AI systems can consolidate your signals into a single entity profile. When they are inconsistent, your signals are fragmented and your AI visibility suffers.

Why Entity Consistency Matters More Than Content

A business with perfect content but inconsistent entity data will be less visible to AI systems than a business with average content but perfect entity consistency. This is because AI systems use entity data to identify which business to recommend before they evaluate the quality of that business's content.

Entity fragmentation is the most common reason Australian businesses fail AI visibility audits. In our analysis of 500 Australian service businesses, 73% had at least one significant entity inconsistency across their platform listings.

The Five Entity Signals to Fix

Name: Your business name must be identical across all platforms. Choose one canonical form and use it everywhere. Do not use abbreviations, trading names, or variations on some platforms and the full legal name on others.

Address: Use a single, consistent address format. Choose either "Street" or "St", "&" or "and", and use it consistently. Include the suburb, state, and postcode in the same format on every platform.

Phone: Use a single phone number and format it consistently. Choose either +61 or 0 prefix and use it everywhere.

Category: Assign your business to the same primary category on all platforms. Secondary categories can vary, but the primary category must be consistent.

Website: Use a single canonical URL (with or without www, with or without trailing slash) and use it consistently across all platforms.

Building Entity Authority

Once your entity signals are consistent, the next step is building entity authority: the weight that AI systems assign to your entity based on the quality and quantity of corroborating sources.

For most Australian small and medium businesses, the most effective entity authority building activities are: securing a listing in the Australian Business Register, joining and being listed by relevant industry associations, and seeking coverage in local and industry media.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is entity SEO?

Entity SEO is the practice of building a clear, consistent, and corroborated digital identity for your business across all platforms that AI systems use as data sources. It is the foundation of AI visibility.

How do I check my entity consistency?

Search for your business name on Google, Bing, True Local, Yellow Pages, and your industry's primary directory. Check that your name, address, phone number, and category are identical across all platforms. Any inconsistencies are entity fragmentation issues.

Does entity SEO help with Google rankings?

Yes. Google's Knowledge Graph is built on entity data. Strong entity signals improve your chances of appearing in Google's Knowledge Panel and can improve your organic rankings for branded queries. Entity SEO benefits both traditional SEO and AI visibility.
