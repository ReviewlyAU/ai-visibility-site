GEO vs SEO: Why Your Marketing Budget is Funding the Wrong Machine

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 7 min read

This page presents a research-angle comparison of GEO and SEO performance data for Australian businesses. For a comprehensive guide to Generative Engine Optimisation as a discipline, see geooptimisation.com.au, the definitive Australian resource on GEO.

The Traffic Bleed Problem

Google AI Overviews now appear for the majority of commercial queries in Australia. When an AI Overview is present, the organic results are pushed below the fold on mobile, which accounts for over 60% of Australian search traffic. Click-through rates for position 1 organic results have fallen significantly in categories where AI Overviews appear.

This is the core problem with a pure SEO strategy in 2026: you can rank number one and still lose the click. The AI Overview answers the query before the user reaches your result.

What GEO Captures That SEO Cannot

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the discipline of optimising for AI-generated answers rather than ranked link positions. A business that appears in a Google AI Overview or a ChatGPT recommendation captures the query at the zero-click level, before the user even considers clicking a link.

For Australian service businesses, this is particularly significant. When someone asks "who is the best electrician in Brisbane?", the AI answer is the decision point. If your business is cited in that answer, you get the lead. If you are not, the user may never scroll to the organic results.

The Data on SEO Traffic Decline

Search Engine Land (January 2026) reported that 37% of consumers now start searches with AI tools rather than Google. For informational queries, AI tool usage is even higher. The businesses most affected are those in categories where AI can provide a confident recommendation: local services, professional services, and product comparisons.

This does not mean SEO is dead. Google search still drives significant traffic for many query types. But for businesses in AI-confident categories, the marginal return on additional SEO investment is declining while the return on GEO investment is increasing.

The Recommended Approach

The most effective strategy for Australian businesses in 2026 is to maintain baseline SEO while building GEO signals. The two disciplines are complementary: strong entity signals and structured data help with both Google rankings and AI citations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SEO dead?

No. SEO remains important for many query types, particularly long-tail informational queries and categories where AI tools do not yet provide confident recommendations. However, for local service businesses, GEO is increasingly more important than incremental SEO improvements.

What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the discipline of optimising content and brand signals for AI-generated answers rather than ranked link positions. For a comprehensive guide, see geooptimisation.com.au.

How do I know if my business is affected by AI Overviews?

Search for your primary service keywords in Google and check whether an AI Overview appears. If it does, check whether your business is cited in it. If it is not, you have a GEO gap that is likely costing you leads.
