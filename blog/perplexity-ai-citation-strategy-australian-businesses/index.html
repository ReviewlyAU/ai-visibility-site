How to Get Cited by Perplexity AI (The Two-Gate Framework)

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 8 min read

Perplexity AI uses a two-gate citation system. Gate 1 is discoverability: can Perplexity's retrieval system find your content? Gate 2 is credibility: does Perplexity's quality filter trust your content enough to cite it? Most Australian businesses fail at Gate 1. Here is how to pass both.

Gate 1: Discoverability

Perplexity uses real-time web retrieval to supplement its language model responses. For your business to be cited, Perplexity's retrieval system must first be able to find content about you. This requires your content to be indexed by Bing (Perplexity's primary search index), not just Google.

Many Australian businesses are well-indexed by Google but poorly indexed by Bing. Check your Bing Webmaster Tools account and submit your sitemap if it is not already indexed. This single step can significantly improve your Perplexity discoverability.

Gate 2: Credibility

Once Perplexity finds your content, it applies a credibility filter. Content that passes this filter is cited; content that does not is ignored. The credibility filter weights: source diversity (is this business mentioned by multiple independent sources?), sentiment (are the mentions positive?), and specificity (does the content provide specific, verifiable information?).

Generic, promotional content fails the credibility filter. Specific, factual content from independent sources passes it. A review that says "Great service, highly recommend" is less credible to Perplexity than a review that says "Fixed our hot water system within 2 hours on a Sunday, charged exactly what was quoted."

The Nine Signals That Matter

Based on analysis of Perplexity citation patterns for Australian businesses, the nine signals with the strongest correlation to citation are: (1) Bing index coverage, (2) review volume on non-Google platforms, (3) Reddit mentions, (4) Quora answers mentioning your business, (5) local news coverage, (6) industry directory listings, (7) specific and detailed review content, (8) consistent entity data across platforms, and (9) FAQ-structured content on your website.

Notably absent from this list: Google PageRank, domain authority, backlink count, and on-page keyword density. These signals matter for Google but have minimal impact on Perplexity citations.

Practical Implementation for Australian Businesses

Start with Bing indexing: submit your sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools and ensure all your key pages are indexed. This takes 30 minutes and is the single highest-leverage Perplexity optimisation available.

Then focus on review specificity: when asking clients for reviews, encourage them to describe the specific problem you solved and the specific outcome you delivered. Specific reviews are more credible to Perplexity's quality filter than generic positive sentiment.

Finally, build Reddit presence: participate genuinely in relevant Australian subreddits. Answer questions in your area of expertise. When appropriate, mention your business in context. Authentic Reddit mentions are among the most powerful Perplexity citation signals available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Perplexity use Google's search index?

No. Perplexity primarily uses Bing's search index for real-time retrieval. Businesses that are well-indexed by Google but not Bing may be invisible to Perplexity. Check and submit to Bing Webmaster Tools.

How important is Reddit for Perplexity citations?

Very important. Reddit is heavily represented in Perplexity's retrieval results. Genuine mentions of your business in relevant Australian subreddits are among the most powerful Perplexity citation signals available.

What type of content does Perplexity prefer to cite?

Specific, factual, independently-sourced content. Generic promotional content fails Perplexity's credibility filter. Detailed reviews, specific case studies, and factual directory listings perform best.
