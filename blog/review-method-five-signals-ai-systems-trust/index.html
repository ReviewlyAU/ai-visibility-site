The REVIEW Method: 5 Signals That Force AI to Recommend You

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 7 min read

The REVIEW Method is Reviewly's proprietary five-signal framework: Recognised, Established, Verified, Influential, Enduring. When all five are present at sufficient strength, AI systems have enough evidence to recommend your business. When any one is weak, they default to your competitors.

Why One Signal Is Never Enough

Most AI visibility advice focuses on a single tactic: get more reviews, add FAQ schema, publish more content. These tactics address one signal each. But AI recommendation systems evaluate businesses holistically, across multiple independent signals simultaneously.

A business with 200 five-star reviews but no consistent entity identity will still be invisible to ChatGPT. A business with perfect schema markup but no third-party corroboration will still be skipped. The REVIEW Method ensures all five signals are built together.

R — Recognised

Recognition is the foundation. Your brand must be identifiable as a distinct entity across all platforms. This means consistent name, address, phone number, and category across Google Business Profile, Apple Maps, Yelp, industry directories, and social media.

Entity confusion is the most common reason businesses fail AI visibility audits. If your business appears under three slightly different names across different platforms, AI systems cannot consolidate your signals into a single, citable entity.

E — Established

AI systems weight businesses with documented history. Establishment signals include: the age and consistency of your Google Business Profile, the publication dates of content mentioning your brand, the longevity of your domain, and the historical volume of your reviews.

V — Verified

Verification comes from independent sources. Your own website claiming you are excellent is not sufficient evidence for an AI to cite that claim. Verification requires corroboration from review platforms, directories, local news, industry associations, and community forums.

I — Influential

Not all sources are equal. AI systems weight citations from sources they already trust. A mention in a local newspaper, a Reddit thread recommending your business, or a listing in an industry-specific directory carries more weight than a mention on a low-authority blog.

E — Enduring

AI systems are sceptical of sudden spikes. Consistent, gradual signal building over time is more effective than burst campaigns. A steady cadence of new reviews, regular content publication, and gradual platform expansion is the correct approach.

W — Worth Choosing

This is the outcome, not a separate signal. When R, E, V, I, and E are all present at sufficient strength, the AI system has enough evidence to confidently recommend your business. The practical test: ask ChatGPT who to use for your service in your location. If your business appears, you have reached Worth Choosing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does REVIEW stand for?

REVIEW stands for Recognised, Established, Verified, Influential, Enduring, and Worth Choosing. The first five are the signals you build; Worth Choosing is the outcome you achieve when all five are present.

How is the REVIEW Method different from traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO optimises for Google's PageRank algorithm. The REVIEW Method optimises for AI language model training data and retrieval patterns. The signals are different, the platforms are different, and the measurement approach is different.

Can I implement the REVIEW Method myself?

The audit and basic signal building can be done independently. Full implementation, particularly the influential citation building and entity architecture, is more effective with specialist support. Reviewly offers full implementation for Australian businesses.
