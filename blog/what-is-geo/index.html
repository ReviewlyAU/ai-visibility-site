What Is GEO? And Why It is Now More Important Than SEO

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 4 min read

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the discipline of optimising content and brand signals for AI-generated answers rather than ranked link positions. For the definitive Australian guide to GEO, visit geooptimisation.com.au.

For the definitive Australian guide to GEO, including industry-specific strategies and city guides, visit geooptimisation.com.au.

GEO in One Paragraph

Traditional SEO optimises for a ranked list of links. GEO optimises for a generated answer. When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews a question, the response is generated, not retrieved. GEO is the practice of building the signals that cause AI systems to generate responses that cite your business.

How GEO Differs from SEO

SEO optimises for Google's PageRank algorithm: backlinks, keyword density, and domain authority. GEO optimises for AI language model training data and retrieval patterns: entity consistency, corroboration, and structured data.

The practical difference: a business can rank #1 on Google and still be invisible to ChatGPT. A business with strong GEO signals may appear in AI answers even if it does not rank highly in traditional search results.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the discipline of optimising content and brand signals for AI-generated answers rather than ranked link positions in traditional search engines.

Is GEO the same as AEO?

GEO and AEO are closely related but distinct. AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) focuses specifically on getting cited in AI answers. GEO is the broader discipline encompassing the full technical and content architecture required for AI visibility.
