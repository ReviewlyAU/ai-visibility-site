The REVIEW Method®: 5 Signals That Force AI to Recommend You

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 8 min read

The REVIEW Method is Reviewly's proprietary five-signal framework for building AI visibility. It defines the exact signals that AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews use to decide whether to recommend a business. When all five signals are present, the AI has enough evidence to confidently cite your brand. When they are absent, it defaults to your competitors.

Why Most AI Visibility Advice Fails

Most advice about "getting ChatGPT to recommend you" focuses on content tactics: write more blog posts, add FAQ schema, optimise for featured snippets. These tactics are not wrong, but they address only one signal in a five-signal system. A business that publishes excellent content but has weak entity recognition, inconsistent NAP data, and no third-party corroboration will still be invisible to AI systems.

The REVIEW Method addresses all five signals simultaneously, which is why it produces results that content-only strategies cannot.

The Five Signals

R — Recognised

AI systems can only recommend businesses they can identify as distinct entities. Recognition means your brand name, category, location, and key attributes appear consistently and unambiguously across the platforms that AI systems use as training data and retrieval sources.

Recognition failures are common: a business operating under slightly different names on different platforms (e.g., "Smith Plumbing" vs "Smith's Plumbing Services" vs "Smith & Sons Plumbing") creates entity confusion that prevents AI systems from consolidating your signals into a single, citable entity.

E — Established

AI systems favour businesses with documented history. Establishment signals include: the age and consistency of your Google Business Profile, the volume and recency of your reviews, the publication dates of content mentioning your brand, and the longevity of your domain.

A business that has been operating for 10 years but only started building digital signals in 2025 will appear "new" to AI systems. Establishment requires retroactive documentation of your history across digital platforms.

V — Verified

AI systems require corroboration. Your own website claiming you are the best plumber in Sydney is not sufficient evidence for an AI to cite that claim. Verification comes from independent sources: review platforms, local directories, industry associations, news mentions, and community forums.

The more independent sources that corroborate your core claims (what you do, where you operate, how good you are), the more confident an AI system will be in recommending you.

I — Influential

Not all sources are equal. AI systems weight citations from sources they already trust. A mention in a local newspaper, a Reddit thread where someone recommends your business, or a listing in an industry-specific directory carries more weight than a mention on a low-authority blog.

Influence is about being cited by the right sources, not just any sources. The REVIEW Method maps the specific platforms and publications that carry the most weight for each industry and location in Australia.

E — Enduring

AI systems are sceptical of sudden spikes. A business that accumulates 50 reviews in a month after years of inactivity, or that suddenly appears across dozens of platforms, triggers quality filters in AI retrieval systems.

Endurance means building signals consistently over time. A steady cadence of new reviews, regular content publication, and gradual expansion of your platform presence is more effective than a burst campaign.

W — Worth Choosing

This is the outcome, not a separate signal. When R, E, V, I, and E are all present at sufficient strength, the AI system has enough evidence to confidently recommend your business. "Worth Choosing" is the state you reach when the other five signals are in place.

The practical test: ask ChatGPT "who should I use for [your service] in [your location]?" If your business appears, you have reached Worth Choosing. If it does not, one or more of the preceding signals is insufficient.

Implementing the REVIEW Method

The REVIEW Method is implemented in three phases:

Phase 1 - Entity Audit (Weeks 1-2): Map your current signal strength across all five dimensions. Identify the gaps between your current state and the threshold required for AI recommendation.

Phase 2 - Signal Construction (Weeks 3-12): Build the missing signals systematically. This includes entity consolidation, review velocity campaigns, platform expansion, and content architecture aligned with AI retrieval patterns.

Phase 3 - Monitoring and Compounding (Ongoing): Track AI citation frequency across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Continue building signals to maintain and strengthen your position as AI systems update their training data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the REVIEW Method?

The REVIEW Method is Reviewly's proprietary five-signal framework for building AI visibility: Recognised, Established, Verified, Influential, Enduring, and Worth Choosing. When all five signals are present, AI systems have enough evidence to confidently recommend your business over competitors.

How long does it take to implement the REVIEW Method?

Initial implementation typically takes 60-90 days to build the core entity signals. AI citation results begin appearing within 3-6 months as AI systems update their training data and retrieval indexes. The full compound effect builds over 12+ months.

Does the REVIEW Method work for small Australian businesses?

Yes. The REVIEW Method was specifically designed for Australian service businesses competing against larger, better-resourced competitors. The framework levels the playing field because AI systems weight corroboration and consistency over raw domain authority.
