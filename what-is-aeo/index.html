What is AEO? (And Why SEO Isn't Enough Anymore)

Updated July 2026 By Reviewly 10 min read

Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) is the discipline of structuring your content, entity data, and brand signals so that AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews understand, trust, and recommend your business when users ask relevant questions. It is distinct from traditional SEO, which optimises for a list of links. AEO optimises for a cited answer.

The Problem with Traditional SEO in 2026

For 20 years, the playbook was simple: rank on page one of Google, get clicks. That playbook is breaking down. Google AI Overviews now appear at the top of the results page for millions of queries, pushing organic links below the fold. ChatGPT has 10.5 million monthly Australian users (Roy Morgan, June 2026). Perplexity is growing. When someone asks an AI "who should I use for [your service] in [your city]?", Google rankings are irrelevant. What matters is whether the AI has enough evidence to cite your business.

Traditional SEO cannot answer that question. AEO can.

How AI Systems Decide Who to Recommend

AI language models do not crawl the web in real time the way Google does. They are trained on large datasets of text, and they generate answers based on patterns in that training data combined with real-time retrieval. For a business to appear in an AI-generated answer, two things need to be true:

  • The AI must recognise your brand as a distinct entity with a clear, consistent identity across multiple sources.
  • The AI must have enough corroborating evidence to confidently recommend you without risking a hallucination or incorrect attribution.

This is why businesses with strong review profiles, consistent NAP data, Wikipedia mentions, and third-party citations appear in AI answers while businesses with better websites and more backlinks do not. The AI is not reading your website the way Google does. It is reconstructing your reputation from distributed signals.

The Five Signals AI Systems Trust

Reviewly's research across thousands of AI citation patterns has identified five core signals that predict whether an AI system will recommend a business. These form the basis of the REVIEW Method.

  • Recognition: Your brand name appears consistently and unambiguously across authoritative platforms. The AI can identify you as a distinct entity.
  • Establishment: You have a documented history. Reviews, mentions, and content that show you have been operating and delivering results over time.
  • Verification: Independent sources corroborate your claims. Not just your own website, but third-party directories, publications, and community platforms.
  • Influence: You are cited by sources that AI systems already trust, including Reddit threads, industry publications, and authoritative directories.
  • Endurance: Your signals are consistent over time. AI systems favour brands with stable, long-term presence over those with sudden spikes of activity.

When all five signals are present, the AI has enough evidence to confidently recommend your business. When they are absent or inconsistent, the AI defaults to the brand it has the most evidence for, which is usually your largest competitor.

Why 88% of Australian Businesses Are Invisible to AI

Research by OmniEclipse (March 2026) found that 88% of businesses do not appear in AI-generated recommendations for their own service categories. The reason is not that their products or services are inferior. It is that they have not built the distributed evidence architecture that AI systems require.

Most Australian small and medium businesses have a website, a Google Business Profile, and some reviews. That is not enough. AI systems are looking for corroboration across a much wider set of sources: industry directories, local news mentions, community forum discussions, structured data, and consistent entity signals across all platforms.

How to Get Started with AEO

The fastest path to AI visibility is to audit your current entity signals and identify the gaps. The REVIEW Method provides a structured framework for doing this. Start by asking: if an AI system tried to reconstruct your business's reputation from publicly available information, what would it find?

If the answer is "not much beyond our own website", that is the problem. The fix is to build corroborating evidence across the platforms that AI systems trust, in a format they can parse.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO)?

Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) is the discipline of structuring your content, entity data, and brand signals so that AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews understand, trust, and recommend your business when users ask relevant questions.

How is AEO different from SEO?

Traditional SEO optimises a single page to rank in a retrieved list of links. SEO targets Google's algorithm; AEO targets AI language models.
